DEFINITION OF

Anacoluthon is derived from the Greek word anakolouthos, which means “lacking sequence.” It is a stylistic device defined as a syntactic deviation, and interruption within a sentence from one structure to another.

ANACOLUTHON

In this interruption, the expected sequence of grammar is absent. The grammatical flow of sentences is interrupted in order to begin more sentences.

NIFTY, EH?

It is employed intentionally, unintentionally, or as a rhetorical device.

MORE ON...

In rhetoric, anacoluthon is also known as a figure of disorder in which the syntax of a sentence does not correlate with whatever is expected. However, it should not to be mixed-up with hyperbaton, which also involves a change in the normal position of words, phrases, and sentences.

ANACOLUTHA (PL)

Anacoluthon is the interruption within a sentence from one construction to another against the expected logical order of the sentence. This change can occur within a sentence or in the form of tense.
